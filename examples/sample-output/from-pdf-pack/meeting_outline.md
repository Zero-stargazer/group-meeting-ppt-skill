## 一句话总结

This paper presents a multiplex rapid assay that combines target amplification, spatial encoding, and fluorescence readout for low-resource pathogen detection.

## 研究问题

Single-target assays are inefficient for co-infection scenarios and require repeated testing.

## 方法路线

- The authors designed primer sets
- mapped targets to spatial codes
- captured fluorescence
- and used threshold rules to call positive reactions.

## 关键发现

- The assay distinguished multiple targets in a single workflow.
- The encoded readout reduced interpretation complexity and worked on simulated samples.
- Overall workflow from sample input to encoded fluorescence readout.
